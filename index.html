Case Study 01 · AI Forecasting — Leadership

Built the AI forecasting engine behind a 131% margin increase.

A weekly AI-generated briefing that transformed fragmented data into actionable commercial insights.

Problem

Leadership had no single view of demand, capacity, or risk. Delivery managers knew their own accounts, but the data needed to diagnose margin pressure lived across four disconnected sources and took days to pull together manually.

Diagnosis — was AI even the right answer?
Could a shared spreadsheet solve this?No — sources updated at different speeds and lived in incompatible formats: Slack threads, PDFs, a tracker, a BI dashboard.
Could a person own this manually?They already did. It took days, by which point leadership was acting on stale data.
Why AI was the right toolSynthesising unstructured qualitative signals (Slack, email) against structured data (spreadsheet, BI) — cross-format reasoning that a human could do, just not fast enough to matter.
The fixNot "add AI to reporting." Keep data collection manual where it already worked. Use Copilot specifically for the one step that needed judgment across formats — turning four disconnected signals into one weekly read leadership could act on.
Mechanism — four real sources, one weekly brief

Case Study 02 · Client Response Copilot — Delivery

Built an AI copilot that answers client's questions 90% faster.

An AI pipeline that retrieves the evidence behind a client question automatically, reasons over it with Microsoft Copilot, and drafts a grounded response.

Problem

Delivery managers and consultants spent up to 30 minutes checking the prepaid tracker, the SOW, and prior email threads before answering a single client billing question. Consultants had to wait for PMs to respond on their behalf — the actual writing was never the cost, locating and validating scattered evidence was.

Diagnosis — was AI even the right answer?
Could we just create a shared FAQ?No — each query is specific to a client's contract, prepaid balance, and email history. A generic FAQ can't reconcile those.
Could we train delivery managers to respond faster?The bottleneck wasn't knowledge — it was the time spent locating and cross-referencing evidence across three disconnected sources before writing anything.
Why AI was the right toolRetrieving and reconciling evidence across a tracker, a contract document, and an email thread — then generating a grounded draft. The human stays in control of every send.
The fixNot "automate client responses." Use Power Automate to retrieve the relevant evidence, use Copilot specifically to reason across it and draft a reply, and keep a human reviewing every word before anything sends.
